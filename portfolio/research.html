Neelima Monjusha Preeti, Trina Chakraborty, Noor Mairukh Khan Arnob, Saiyara Mahmud, Azmine Toushik Wasi (2025). Ensemble Approach for Misogyny Detection in Memes Using Pre-trained Text and Vision Transformers, Fifth Workshop on Speech, Vision, and Language Technologies for Dravidian Languages (DravidianLangTech-2025). Published 

This study focuses on misogynistic memes in Tamil and Malayalam, two Dravidian languages with limited resources. It uses computer vision and natural language processing for multi-modal detection, using CLIP embeddings for vision and BERT models trained on hate speech datasets for text. The integrated approach effectively captures the unique characteristics of misogynistic memes, achieving competitive performance with a Macro F1 Score of 0.7800 for Tamil and 0.8748 for Malayalam.

Neelima Monjusha Preeti, Dr. Md. Imdadul Islam (2024). Detection of Brain Tumor from MRI Image under Deep learning and Bag of Features Algorithm [Master’s Thesis]

Accurate disease detection from medical images is essential in modern healthcare, with machine learning and deep learning techniques minimizing human error. This research focuses on detecting brain tumors in MRIs using preprocessing methods and pseudo-coloring, followed by model training with CNN and LSTM. The Bag-of-Features (BoF) algorithm is applied to the preprocessed dataset.

Emrul Hasan, Chen Ding, Sajib Saha, Neelima Monjusha Preeti and Abdul Halim (2024).Accommodation Review Ranking for Tourism Recommendation (RecTour 2024), October 18th, 2024, co-located with the 18th ACM Conference on Recommender Systems, Bari, Italy. Published 

Reviews significantly impact users' decisions, with top reviews often shaping interaction experiences. Traditional ranking methods—such as score-based, helpfulness votes, and time-based rankings—have limitations, including sparse data and inability to fully capture user preferences. The proposed method addresses these issues by using similarity-based ranking through user and item profiles, feature extraction via Sentence Transformers, and Cosine Similarity for sorting. This approach ranks reviews based on their relevance to the user’s profile and preferences, enhancing recommendation accuracy.

Neelima Monjusha Preeti, Faiza Feroz, Dr. Md. Imdadul Islam (2023). Handover Detection of WAN using FIS. Jahangirnagar University Journal of Electronics and Computer Science. Published 

In high-speed WAN scenarios, frequent handovers occur due to smaller cell coverage in 4G/5G compared to 3G. To reduce unnecessary handovers and control plane overhead while preserving user plane QoS, this paper introduces a Fuzzy Inference System (FIS) using four fuzzy variables—velocity, SNR, SINR, and acceleration—each with 3–5 linguistic values. Around 200 fuzzy rules link crisp and fuzzy inputs. Four defuzzification methods—Centroid, Bisection, MOM, and LOM—are evaluated via tables and surface plots, all achieving over 92% accuracy.

Neelima Monjusha Preeti, Sushmoy Barua, Anup Majumdar (2024). Feature Ranking for Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) Detection using XAI from Ultrasound Images [In Preparation]

Feature ranking for Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) detection using Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) techniques provides insights into which ultrasound image features are most indicative of the condition. By applying XAI to deep learning models, critical features such as ovarian volume, follicle count, and stromal area are ranked based on their importance in diagnosing PCOS. This approach enhances model transparency, helping clinicians understand the reasoning behind AI-based predictions. Accurate feature ranking with XAI supports more reliable and interpretable PCOS detection, aiding early diagnosis and personalized treatment planning.

Neelima Monjusha Preeti, Rubaiya Reza Sohana , Sabrina Afrin Toma (2021). Building Rich Community with DHR System and using ML for Decision Making ensuring Enriched Health Facility and eradicating Health Myths [1st International Poster Competition 2021]

Developing countries face healthcare challenges, including doctor shortages, financial constraints, lack of digital records, and limited resources. This proposal introduces a Digital Health Record (DHR) system, supported by regional health workers, essential devices, and solar power, to securely connect healthcare providers. Implemented with PHP and MySQL, the DHR will maintain patient histories, enable data sharing, and leverage machine learning for predictive modeling and early warnings of high-risk diseases. By detecting regional health trends, minimizing treatment delays, and reducing medication errors, the DHR aims to strengthen community health outcomes, aligning with SDG-13.
